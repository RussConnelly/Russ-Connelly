Research

My current research focuses on the detection of UK seahorse species using passive acoustic monitoring (PAM) and environmental DNA (eDNA). This method enables us to monitor these elusive creatures in their natural habitat with minimal disturbance, helping to inform conservation efforts.

Education

PhD in Marine Biology, University of Essex (2023)
Specializing in UK seahorse detection using PAM and eDNA techniques.

MSD Marine Biology, University of Essex (2021-2023)
Investigated fish community composition in the Wakatobi Marine National Park and studied Ice Fish nests in the Weddell Sea using ROV data.

BSc Marine Biology, University of Essex (2018-2021)
Graduated with First-Class Honors. Awarded the Environmental and Conservation Prize for exceptional academic performance.
