Projects

PhD - Using new smart tools (eDNA and acoustics) to detect cryptic UK seahorse species:
  • This PhD project is looking to explore novel ways to accurately record seahorse presence within English coastal habitats. Current methods for detecting seahorses require significant funding, are time-consuming, have significant health and safety considerations, and need specialist training to conduct, which will inherently produce biases in sampling cryptic species. The rationale behind this multi-faceted approach is to comprehensively detect for presence of seahorses within the heterogeneous habitats of the UK.
  • Supervisors for this project include: Dr. Michelle Taylor, Dr. Boyd Mckew, Dr. Lucy Woodall (University of Exeter/Nekton), Dr. Hazel Selley (Natural England), and Dr. Sophie Nedelec (University of Exeter).

MRes Maintained Cryonotothenioid Nesting Sites:
  • This project is part of the data collected on the WSE2019 expedition to the Western Weddell Sea in search for Ernest Shakelton's ship.
  • Video data was recorded onboard the ROV Lassie at around 450 metres deep.
  • A finding of maintained Cryonotothenioid nesting sites due to a recent algal bloom die off.
  • Supervisors for this project include: Dr. Michelle Taylor, and Dr. Lucy Woodall (University of Exeter/Nekton)

    Seahorses found!

    Read More

    Seahorses found in Studland Bay! Last week, we had an incredible find in Studland Bay – three beautiful seahorses, all from the species Hippocampus guttulatus! Just as we were getting ready to celebrate, the unforecasted Easterly winds decided to crash the party, turning the visibility for the rest of the week. Thankfully, hydrophones and hydromoths don’t need clear waters to do their job. So, while the winds may have ruined my eyes, we’re still hopeful to detect the seahorses’ feeding clicks throughout the week with our ears. Stay tuned for more updates!
